This is my old webpage. Here is my new webpage.

I am doing my PhD under the supervision of Jörg Lücke at the TU Berlin. My interests were nurtured in the machine learning group at the MPI in Tuebingen where I worked on kernel methods and have since ventured to the probabilistic side using Bayesian modelling, and now sometimes even combine them. My primary applications include neuroscience and image processing. I am currently at TU Berlin and putting the finishing touches on my PhD thesis about large-scale approximate inference in probabilistic models.

I completed my M.S. in Computer Science (Informatik) at the Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics dept. Schölkopf (Empirical Inference) with the Universität Tübingen, on semi-supervised Kernel CCA applied to fMRI data with supervision from Andreas Bartels, Matthew Blaschko, Arthur Gretton, and Christoph Lampert. Here is my old homepage at the MPI.
