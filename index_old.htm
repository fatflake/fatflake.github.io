I am doing my PhD under the supervision of Jörg Lücke in the Computational Neuroscience and Machine Learning Group. My main research interest is machine learning; its theory and applications, specifically, currently the development of approximate inference methods that can be efficiently scaled to high-dimensional data, and application to problems in neuroscience.

  • Here is my CV and old homepage at the Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics, where I completed my M.S. in Computer Science (Informatik) with the Universität Tübingen, on semi-supervised Kernel CCA applied to fMRI data with supervision from Matthew Blaschko, Arthur Gretton, and Christoph Lampert.
